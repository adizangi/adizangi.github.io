The subjunctive

The subjunctive is used to express something that is not necessarily factual or real. Compare the following:

Juan estudia mucho y recibe buenas notas.

Juan studies a lot and gets good grades.

Los padres de Juan quieren que él estudie mucho y que reciba buenas notas.

Juan's parents want him to study a lot and get good grades.

The information in the first sentence is factual and for this reason is in the indicative. The second sentence tells what may happen. Just because Juan's parents want him to study more doesn't mean he will. For this reason it's in the subjunctive.
